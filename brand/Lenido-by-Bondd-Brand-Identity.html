The stay

Le NIDO by Bondd is the short-term rental expression of Bondd Homes. It fills the gap between overpriced and disappointing — the premium stay that doesn't require premium sacrifice. Every space is designed, managed, and priced for the 20–40 Lagos professional who knows good design when they see it and won't overpay for a mediocre experience.

A significant portion of Lenido guests are diaspora Nigerians — people who've lived with international standards and want that same quality when they come home. The brand must speak to both without condescending to either.

02 — Color

Warm.
Fresh.
Grounded.

The palette is drawn entirely from the Bondd Homes family. Three Bondd colors are excluded — Neon Orange, Vibrant Blue, Outer Green — because they carry the parent brand's high-energy development identity. The remaining seven form Lenido's complete world.

Blue Royale is the dark foundation — Bondd's distinctive deep navy, used for all dominant backgrounds and compositions. Unlike plain black, Royale is uniquely Bondd — it creates the visual connection to the parent brand that makes Lenido unmistakably part of the family. Ambient Peach is the emotional signature, and Blush Blue brings freshness — morning light, clean spaces, pool energy. Black is reserved for body text and fine details only.

What changes is which colors fill those shapes, and the margin increases from Bondd's 7% to Lenido's 9% — a small but deliberate step that creates a little more breathing room, fitting a hospitality brand where the guest should feel at ease rather than sold to.

04 — Tone of Voice

Say it like you live here.

Copy should feel like it was written by someone who lives in Lagos, travels often, and knows good taste. Direct, warm, confident. Not corporate. Not trying too hard.

✓ Lenido
"Stay like you mean it."

Three words. Speaks to intention, not features. Works on a billboard, a post, a website header.

✕ Not Lenido
"Experience unparalleled luxury accommodation in the heart of Lagos Island."

Generic hotel language. Could be written by anyone. No Lagos energy, no personality.

✓ Lenido
"Your Lagos base. No compromises."

Talks to the diaspora guest directly. "Base" implies ownership for the duration. "No compromises" addresses the fear head-on.

✕ Not Lenido
"Affordable prices for every budget!"

Undermines the premium positioning entirely. Lenido is great value for what it is — never "cheap." Never lead with price anxiety.

✓ Lenido
"Wake up to this."

Paired with a great interior shot, it closes without a single adjective. The image earns it, the copy confirms it.

✕ Not Lenido
"Book now and enjoy our world-class amenities and premium facilities!"

Exclamation marks, jargon, listing features. The Lenido guest knows what good looks like — show them, don't list it.
